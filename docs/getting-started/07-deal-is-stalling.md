# Chapter 7: This Deal is Stalling

*The Kohl's deal has been quiet for three weeks. Sarah Chen stopped responding to emails. Your champion in Marketing Ops — the director who loved the demo — hasn't booked the technical review. No meetings, no replies, no movement. Your manager is asking for a forecast update. Time to shake it loose.*

## The goal

By the end of this chapter, you'll know how AgentPilot surfaces stalling deals, how to run a re-engagement play using a fresh news hook, and how to create a champion enablement page that arms your internal advocate with executive-ready content.

## Prerequisites

Kohl's is in your account list with an established engagement history — emails sent, sales pages created, contacts in the buying group (Chapters 2-6).

---

## Step 1: See the stale deal surface

Open your **Dashboard**. The **Next Best Actions** card highlights stale accounts automatically.

The system tracks engagement at the account level. When there's been no activity — no email opens, no page visits, no meetings — for 21+ days, it flags the account:

> **Kohl's — Marketing**
> "No engagement in 23 days. Last activity: email opened June 12."
> Recommended play: Re-Engagement

You didn't have to remember that Kohl's went dark. The system tracked it and told you.

[Screenshot: Next Best Actions showing Kohl's flagged as a stale deal with re-engagement recommendation]

---

## Step 2: Find a fresh hook

Before reaching out, you need a reason to email — not "just checking in." Open Kohl's company page → **Signals** tab.

Even though Sarah went quiet, the world didn't stop. You might find:

- A new earnings call where the CEO mentioned "digital transformation" priorities
- A competitor acquisition that changes the landscape
- A job posting for "Marketing Automation Engineer" — they're investing in the stack
- An industry report that references Kohl's competitive position

Pick the freshest, most relevant signal. Let's say:

> **Industry News**
> "Kohl's CEO announces $50M investment in digital customer experience in Q3 earnings call"
> *Relevance: 8/10*

That's your hook. Not "I'm following up" — but "I saw something relevant and thought of you."

[Screenshot: Signals tab showing the earnings call signal with relevance score]

---

## Step 3: Run the re-engagement play

Click **Run Play** on the signal, or go to **Content** tab → **Create Content** → select **Re-Engagement** as the play type.

The re-engagement play loads:
- Full account context (research, stack, objections)
- The stale engagement history
- The fresh signal as a news hook
- Your messaging framework

It generates:

### The email

- **Opens with the signal** — references the $50M digital investment from the earnings call
- **Bridges to relevance** — connects the investment to the marketing team's needs
- **Doesn't guilt-trip** — no "I haven't heard from you" energy
- **Offers new value** — maybe a relevant case study that just published, or a new feature that addresses an objection Sarah raised
- **Low-friction CTA** — "Happy to send over a 2-page summary of how [similar retailer] approached this. Want me to?"

The key: the email has a reason to exist beyond the deal. It's about their business, not your quota.

[Screenshot: Re-engagement email using the earnings call as a news hook]

---

## Step 4: Consider the champion route

Maybe the problem isn't Sarah. Maybe Sarah is bought in but can't get budget from her CFO. In that case, you need to arm your champion with content they can forward up.

Run the **Champion Enablement** play instead (or in addition to re-engagement).

### How champion enablement works

The play generates a page designed for the internal sale — when your champion needs to convince their executive:

- **Outcome-driven** — Not "here's what our product does" but "here's the business impact for Kohl's"
- **Executive framing** — Revenue impact, competitive positioning, risk of inaction
- **Kohl's-specific** — References their initiatives, their industry, their competitive landscape
- **Shareable** — A clean URL your champion can forward without it feeling like a vendor pitch

This is a fundamentally different page from the sales pages in Chapter 2. That page sold features to a practitioner. This page sells outcomes to a budget holder.

## Step 5: Generate the champion page

From the play execution screen, select:
- **Play type:** Champion Enablement
- **Target:** Marketing buying group at Kohl's
- **Product:** MC Next

The system generates a sales page with sections like:

- **Hero** — "Modernizing Marketing Technology: The Business Case for Kohl's"
- **Value props** — Framed as business outcomes: "Reduce campaign launch time from 2 weeks to 3 days," "Eliminate $200K/year in third-party data sync costs"
- **Comparison** — Current state vs. future state (not your product vs. competitor)
- **Case study** — A retailer at similar scale who made the transition, with revenue impact numbers
- **Risk of inaction** — What happens if they don't move: competitive gap widens, MCE support timeline shrinks
- **CTA** — "Request a 30-minute executive briefing"

The page is live at `/go/kohls-marketing-executive-case` and can be shared directly with your champion.

[Screenshot: Generated champion enablement sales page with outcome-driven sections]

---

## Step 6: Send the champion the link

You have a few options:

1. **Email your champion directly** — "Here's a page you can share with Sarah and the CFO. It covers the business case we discussed — specific to Kohl's, not a generic pitch deck."

2. **Share as a briefing** — Use the briefing share feature from Chapter 5 if you want code-protected access.

3. **Include in a warm handoff** — If you're introducing your champion to an executive sponsor on your side, the page serves as the pre-read.

The point: you're giving your champion ammunition, not homework. The page does the selling they'd otherwise have to do with a slide deck they'd have to build.

[Screenshot: Email to champion with link to the executive case page]

---

## Step 7: Log and track

After sending:

- The re-engagement email and champion page are logged on the **Engagement** tab
- Activity is tracked: opens, clicks, page visits
- If Sarah clicks through to the champion enablement page, you'll see it — and that tells you she forwarded it

You can set a follow-up reminder. If there's still no movement in another week, you'll see Kohl's in Next Best Actions again, this time with updated context.

## What just happened

A stalling deal got two distinct interventions:

1. **Stale deal detection** — The system flagged Kohl's after 21 days of silence
2. **Fresh news hook** — Instead of "just checking in," you led with a relevant earnings call signal
3. **Re-engagement email** — The outreach had a reason to exist beyond your pipeline
4. **Champion enablement** — You created an executive-ready page your champion can forward without feeling like they're selling for you
5. **Executive framing** — The champion page speaks in outcomes and risk, not features and specs

Two plays for two problems: the re-engagement play for the direct contact who went dark, and the champion enablement play for the internal sale that's stuck.

---

## Vocabulary box

Terms introduced in this chapter:

- **Re-engagement play** — An outreach motion for stale deals that uses a fresh signal as a news hook to restart the conversation. Triggered automatically when accounts have no engagement for 21+ days.
- **Champion enablement** — A play that generates executive-facing content for your internal champion to share upward. Framed around business outcomes and risk of inaction, not product features.
- **Executive framing** — Content positioning optimized for budget holders: revenue impact, competitive risk, cost of inaction, ROI projections. Distinct from practitioner-facing feature content.
- **Stale deal** — An account with no engagement activity (email opens, page visits, meetings) for a configurable period (default: 21 days).
