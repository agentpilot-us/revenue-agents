# Chapter 3: I Need to Bump My Sales 35%

*Quarter starts in two weeks. Your manager pulls you into a 1:1 and says: "We need 35% pipeline growth this quarter. Where's it coming from?" You don't have an answer yet. Let's fix that.*

## The goal

By the end of this chapter, you'll have a prioritized territory plan in your Sales Map, new accounts researched with buying groups identified, product fit scored across departments, and personalized outreach drafted for your highest-value opportunity.

## Prerequisites

You've completed [Chapter 1](./01-setting-up-your-command-center.md). Your product catalog and content library are loaded.

---

## Step 1: Check your Next Best Actions

Open the **Dashboard**. The **Next Best Actions** card is front and center.

This card scans your accounts and surfaces what needs attention:

- Accounts with buying groups that don't have a sales page yet → "Open new buying group"
- Departments with no contacts → "Find contacts"
- Accounts with upcoming events → "Send event invite"
- Accounts with stale engagement → "Re-engage"
- Roadmap plans ready to execute → links to specific actions

Think of it as a prioritized to-do list that updates itself. The recommendations come from your roadmap targets, signal data, and engagement history.

[Screenshot: Dashboard showing Next Best Actions card with Recommended Plays]

---

## Step 2: Open your Sales Map

Click **Your Sales Map** in the left sidebar. This is your strategic planning view.

### What you see:

- **Roadmap type** — The strategy frame: enterprise expansion, channel influence, or value of data. Pick the one that matches your motion.
- **Objective** — Your high-level goal (e.g., "Grow pipeline 35% in Q2 by expanding Data Cloud footprint in retail vertical")
- **Target map** — Your accounts and their buying groups, organized by stage
- **Phased plans** — AI-generated action plans grouped by priority phase

[Screenshot: Sales Map page showing target map and phased plans]

### Set your objective

Click **Edit** on the roadmap objective. Write something specific:

> "Expand Data Cloud and MC Next into 8 new buying groups across existing retail accounts. Generate $2.4M in qualified pipeline by end of Q2."

The objective frames every AI recommendation. Make it concrete.

[Screenshot: Roadmap objective editor with a specific quarterly goal]

---

## Step 3: Add target accounts

You need accounts on the map. Two options:

### Option A: Import from Salesforce

If you connected Salesforce in Chapter 1, click **Import Accounts**. Select the accounts from your book that have the most expansion potential.

### Option B: Add manually

Click **Add Target** → enter the company name and website. AgentPilot creates the account record.

Let's add **Target** (a national retailer) and **Macy's** as new targets.

[Screenshot: Adding a new target account to the Sales Map]

---

## Step 4: Run AI research on an account

Open Target's company page. If this is a new account, the Overview tab will be sparse. Fix that.

Click **Run Research**. The AI uses Exa to:

- Pull company overview, recent news, and financial data
- Identify key business initiatives
- Discover organizational structure
- Find executive hires and departures
- Map the technology stack

Within a minute, the Overview tab populates with:

- **Business overview** — What the company does, recent strategy shifts
- **Key initiatives** — What they're investing in right now
- **Buying groups** — Departments identified as potential buyers (e.g., Marketing, E-commerce, IT)

[Screenshot: Company Overview tab after AI research, showing business overview and buying groups]

---

## Step 5: Understand product fit

With buying groups identified, you need to know which products fit which departments.

The system runs **product fit scoring** using your product catalog and the account's context. For each buying group:

- Which of your products solve their problems?
- What's the existing stack overlap?
- What framing should the system use (expansion, upgrade, net new)?

You can view this on the **Buying Groups** tab. Each department shows:

- **Products matched** — which products fit this group
- **Fit reasoning** — why the AI thinks there's a match
- **Framing** — how content should be positioned

For example, Target's Marketing Ops team runs an older email platform → MC Next fits as a **net new** play. Their E-commerce team already uses Data Cloud → Data Cloud expansion fits as an **expansion** play.

[Screenshot: Buying Groups tab showing product fit scores per department]

---

## Step 6: Draft personalized outreach

Pick the highest-fit buying group — say Target's Marketing Ops.

Go to the **Content** tab → **Create Content**. Select:
- **Content type:** Email
- **Target:** Marketing Ops department
- **Product:** MC Next

The AI generates an email that:
- References Target's specific business initiatives (from research)
- Leads with the pain points relevant to Marketing Ops
- Uses the net-new framing (since Target doesn't have MC Next)
- Pulls in a case study from a similar retailer in your content library
- Closes with a CTA to a personalized sales page

Review it. Adjust the tone. Send it or save it for later.

[Screenshot: Generated outreach email for Target Marketing Ops with product fit context]

---

## Step 7: Set a deal objective

Back on Target's company page, go to the **Overview** tab.

You can set a **deal objective** — a specific goal for this account:

> "Expand Data Cloud footprint to Marketing Ops and E-commerce. Close MC Next for Marketing Ops by Q2. Target deal size: $450K."

This objective:
- Anchors all AI-generated content for this account
- Informs the Next Best Actions prioritization
- Shows up in your Prep Me talking points (Chapter 5)

[Screenshot: Deal objective set on the company Overview tab]

---

## Step 8: Review your phased plans

Go back to the **Sales Map**. Your new targets now show up in the target map. The system generates **phased plans** — prioritized sequences of actions:

### Phase 1: Quick wins
- Accounts where you have existing relationships + strong product fit
- Actions: create sales pages, send feature announcements

### Phase 2: Build pipeline
- Accounts with good product fit but fewer contacts
- Actions: find contacts, open new buying groups, generate intro content

### Phase 3: Long-term plays
- Net-new accounts requiring multi-touch engagement
- Actions: research, signal monitoring, event plays

Each plan has:
- **Urgency score** — based on signals, deal stage, and time sensitivity
- **Content type** — email, event invite, presentation, case study, ROI deck, talking points
- **Status** — pending, approved, executed, or dismissed

You can approve plans to move them to execution, or dismiss ones that don't fit your strategy.

[Screenshot: Phased plans view showing Phase 1 quick wins with urgency scores]

## What just happened

You went from "I have no idea where 35% comes from" to a concrete, prioritized plan:

1. **Next Best Actions** showed you where to start
2. **Sales Map** gave you the strategic framework
3. **AI research** filled in account context you'd normally spend hours gathering
4. **Product fit** told you exactly which products go where
5. **Outreach** was drafted with full account context — not a generic template
6. **Phased plans** organized your territory into actionable waves

The 35% isn't a mystery anymore. It's a list of specific buying groups, specific products, and specific outreach — ordered by priority.

## Vocabulary box

Terms introduced in this chapter:

- **Sales Map** — Your strategic command center (**Your Sales Map** in the sidebar). Defines which accounts to target, which products to push, and what actions to take — organized into phased plans with urgency scoring. Also houses signal configuration and action mappings.
- **Roadmap target** — An account added to your Sales Map for strategic tracking, with stage (Active Program, Expansion Target, Emerging, etc.) and associated contacts
- **Phased plans** — AI-generated action sequences grouped by priority: quick wins first, then pipeline building, then long-term plays. Each plan has an urgency score, content type, and status.
- **Deal objective** — A specific, written goal for an account (e.g., "Close MC Next for Marketing Ops by Q2"). Anchors all AI-generated content for the account.
- **Product fit** — How well a product matches a buying group's needs, scored by the AI using product profiles, account research, and existing stack data
- **Buying groups** — Departments or teams within an account that make purchasing decisions together (e.g., "Marketing Ops at Target"). Visible on the Buying Groups tab with value propositions, use cases, and key initiative tags.
- **Contacts** — People within buying groups, organized on the Contacts tab. Each contact has a buying role (economic buyer, technical evaluator, champion, influencer), enrichment status, and engagement status. Discovered via **Find Contacts** or imported from Salesforce.
- **AI research** — Automated company research using Exa: business overview, initiatives, org structure, technology stack, and recent signals
- **Content tab** — The hub for generating outreach. Select a channel (Email, LinkedIn InMail, LinkedIn Post, Slack DM, SMS, Sales Page, or Presentation), pick a buying group, and the AI generates personalized content using full account context.
